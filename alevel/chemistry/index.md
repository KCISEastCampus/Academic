## 1. âš›ï¸ Atomic Structure (3.1.1)

### 1.1 Fundamental Particles
* An atom consists of a dense **nucleus** containing protons and neutrons, surrounded by electrons.
* **Relative Mass** and **Charge**:

| Particle | Relative Charge | Relative Mass |
|---|---|---|
| **Proton** | $+1$ | $1$ |
| **Neutron** | $0$ | $1$ |
| **Electron** | $-1$ | $\approx \frac{1}{1840}$ |

* **Mass number ($A$)** = number of protons + neutrons.
* **Atomic (proton) number ($Z$)** = number of protons (defines the element).

### 1.2 Mass Number and Isotopes
* **Isotopes**: Atoms of the same element (same $Z$) with different numbers of **neutrons** (different $A$).
* **Time of Flight (TOF) Mass Spectrometer**:
    1.  **Ionisation**: Sample converted to positive ions (e.g., via Electron Impact or Electrospray).
    2.  **Acceleration**: Ions accelerated by an electric field to constant kinetic energy ($KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$).
    3.  **Ion Drift**: Lighter ions ($\uparrow v$) travel faster than heavier ions ($\downarrow v$).
    4.  **Detection**: Ions hit the detector, generating a current. Size of current $\propto$ abundance.
* **Calculation**:
  $$\text{Relative Atomic Mass } (A_r) = \frac{\sum (\text{isotopic mass} \times \text{relative abundance})}{\text{total relative abundance}}$$

### 1.3 Electron Configuration
* Electrons fill orbitals in order of increasing energy: **1s, 2s, 2p, 3s, 3p, 4s, 3d, 4p...**
    * *Note*: $4s$ fills before $3d$ but also empties before $3d$ when forming ions.
* **Exceptions**:
    * **Chromium (Cr)**: $[\mathrm{Ar}] 3d^5 4s^1$ (half-full d-subshell stability).
    * **Copper (Cu)**: $[\mathrm{Ar}] 3d^{10} 4s^1$ (full d-subshell stability).
* **Ionisation Energy (IE)**:
    * **First IE**: $\mathrm{X(g)} \rightarrow \mathrm{X^+(g)} + \mathrm{e^-}$
    * **Trends**:
        * **Down a Group**: Decreases (more shielding, larger radius).
        * **Across a Period**: Generally increases (higher nuclear charge, similar shielding).
        * *Dips in Period 3*: $\mathrm{Mg \rightarrow Al}$ ($3p$ higher energy than $3s$), $\mathrm{P \rightarrow S}$ (repulsion in paired $3p$ orbital).

## 2. âš–ï¸ Amount of Substance (3.1.2)

### 2.1 The Mole and Avogadro
* **Avogadro Constant ($N_A$)**: $6.02 \times 10^{23} \ \mathrm{mol^{-1}}$.
* **Moles ($n$)**:
  $$n = \frac{\text{Mass } (m)}{M_r} \quad \text{or} \quad n = \text{Conc } (c) \times \text{Vol } (V)$$

### 2.2 The Ideal Gas Equation
$$PV = nRT$$
* **Units are crucial**:
    * $P$ = Pressure in **Pa** (kPa $\times 1000$).
    * $V$ = Volume in **mÂ³** (dm$^3 \div 1000$; cm$^3 \div 10^6$).
    * $T$ = Temperature in **K** ($^\circ$C $+ 273$).
    * $R$ = $8.31 \ \mathrm{J\ K^{-1}\ mol^{-1}}$.

### 2.3 Empirical & Molecular Formula
* **Empirical Formula**: Simplest whole number ratio of atoms of each element in a compound.
* **Molecular Formula**: Actual number of atoms of each element in a molecule.

### 2.4 Balanced Equations & Efficiency
* **Percentage Yield**:
  $$\frac{\text{Actual Yield}}{\text{Theoretical Yield}} \times 100$$
* **Percentage Atom Economy**:
  $$\frac{\text{Molecular mass of desired product}}{\text{Sum of molecular masses of all reactants}} \times 100$$

## 3. ðŸ”— Bonding (3.1.3)

### 3.1 Bonding Types
* **Ionic**: Electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions (lattice). High m.p., conducts when molten/aq.
* **Covalent**: Shared pair of electrons.
    * **Dative (Coordinate)**: Both electrons in the bond come from one atom (e.g., $\mathrm{NH_4^+}$).
* **Metallic**: Attraction between lattice of positive metal ions and sea of delocalised electrons. Malleable, ductile, good conductors.

### 3.2 Shapes of Molecules (VSEPR)
* **Repulsion Strength**: Lone Pair-Lone Pair > Lone Pair-Bonding Pair > Bonding Pair-Bonding Pair.

| Bonding Pairs | Lone Pairs | Shape | Angle | Example |
|:-:|:-:|---|---|---|
| 2 | 0 | Linear | $180^\circ$ | $\mathrm{BeCl_2}$ |
| 3 | 0 | Trigonal Planar | $120^\circ$ | $\mathrm{BF_3}$ |
| 2 | 1 | Bent / V-shape | $117.5^\circ$ | $\mathrm{SO_2}$ |
| 4 | 0 | Tetrahedral | $109.5^\circ$ | $\mathrm{CH_4}$ |
| 3 | 1 | Trigonal Pyramidal | $107^\circ$ | $\mathrm{NH_3}$ |
| 2 | 2 | Bent / V-shape | $104.5^\circ$ | $\mathrm{H_2O}$ |
| 5 | 0 | Trigonal Bipyramidal | $90^\circ, 120^\circ$ | $\mathrm{PCl_5}$ |
| 6 | 0 | Octahedral | $90^\circ$ | $\mathrm{SF_6}$ |

### 3.3 Intermolecular Forces (IMFs)
1.  **Van der Waals (Induced dipole-dipole)**: Weakest. Occur in all molecules. Strength increases with number of electrons (surface area).
2.  **Permanent Dipole-Dipole**: Between polar molecules (difference in electronegativity).
3.  **Hydrogen Bonding**: Strongest IMF. Requires **H** bonded directly to **N, O, or F**.
    * *Effect*: Higher m.p./b.p. than expected (e.g., $\mathrm{H_2O}$ vs $\mathrm{H_2S}$). Ice is less dense than water due to open lattice structure.

## 4. âš¡ Energetics (3.1.4)

### 4.1 Enthalpy Change ($\Delta H$)
* **Exothermic ($\Delta H < 0$)**: Heat lost to surroundings (temp rises).
* **Endothermic ($\Delta H > 0$)**: Heat taken from surroundings (temp falls).
* **Standard Enthalpy of Formation ($\Delta_f H^\circ$)**: Enthalpy change when **1 mole** of a compound is formed from its elements under standard conditions.
* **Standard Enthalpy of Combustion ($\Delta_c H^\circ$)**: Enthalpy change when **1 mole** of a substance is burned completely in oxygen.

### 4.2 Calorimetry
$$q = mc\Delta T$$
* $\Delta H = -q / n$ (Check signs carefully!).

**Required Practical 2 ðŸŒ¡ï¸**: Measure an enthalpy change (e.g., Hess's Law cycles).

### 4.3 Hess's Law
* Enthalpy change is independent of the route taken.
    * **Formation Cycle**: Arrows point **UP** from elements.
        $$\Delta H = \Sigma \Delta_f H^\circ (\text{products}) - \Sigma \Delta_f H^\circ (\text{reactants})$$
    * **Combustion Cycle**: Arrows point **DOWN** to combustion products ($\mathrm{CO_2} + \mathrm{H_2O}$).
        $$\Delta H = \Sigma \Delta_c H^\circ (\text{reactants}) - \Sigma \Delta_c H^\circ (\text{products})$$

### 4.4 Mean Bond Enthalpies
$$\Delta H = \Sigma (\text{Bonds Broken}) - \Sigma (\text{Bonds Formed})$$
* Breaking bonds is **endothermic** (+). Making bonds is **exothermic** (-).

## 5. ðŸ”„ Oxidation, Reduction & Redox (3.1.5)

### 5.1 Definitions
* **Oxidation**: Loss of electrons / Increase in oxidation state.
* **Reduction**: Gain of electrons / Decrease in oxidation state.
* **Oxidising Agent**: Accepts electrons (gets reduced).
* **Reducing Agent**: Donates electrons (gets oxidised).

### 5.2 Oxidation States
* **Rules**:
    * Elements (e.g., $\mathrm{Cl_2}, \mathrm{Na}$): $0$.
    * Group 1: $+1$; Group 2: $+2$; Al: $+3$.
    * F: $-1$ always.
    * O: $-2$ (except in peroxides $-1$ and with F).
    * H: $+1$ (except in metal hydrides $-1$).
* Sum of states in a compound = 0; in an ion = charge on ion.

### 5.3 Half-Equations
* Balance atoms $\rightarrow$ Balance O using $\mathrm{H_2O}$ $\rightarrow$ Balance H using $\mathrm{H^+}$ $\rightarrow$ Balance charge using $\mathrm{e^-}$.
* **Example (Manganate VII in acid)**:
    $$\mathrm{MnO_4^-} + 8\mathrm{H^+} + 5\mathrm{e^-} \rightarrow \mathrm{Mn^{2+}} + 4\mathrm{H_2O}$$

## 6. ðŸ§­ Periodicity (3.2.1)

### 6.1 Trends in Period 3 (Na to Ar)
* **Atomic Radius**: **Decreases**. (Proton number increases, shielding constant $\rightarrow$ stronger attraction).
* **First Ionisation Energy**: **Increases** general trend.
* **Melting Points**:
    * **Na, Mg, Al**: High (Metallic bonding, strength increases with charge).
    * **Si**: Very High (Giant Covalent / Macromolecular).
    * **P, S, Cl**: Low (Simple Molecular, VdW forces). $\mathrm{S_8 > P_4 > Cl_2}$ due to size.
    * **Ar**: Lowest (Monatomic).

## 7. ðŸ‘‘ Group 2 (Alkaline Earth Metals) (3.2.2)

### 7.1 Trends Down the Group
* **Atomic Radius**: Increases.
* **First Ionisation Energy**: Decreases.
* **Melting Point**: Decreases (Metallic bond gets weaker as ion size increases).

### 7.2 Reactions & Solubility
* **With Water**: $\mathrm{M} + 2\mathrm{H_2O} \rightarrow \mathrm{M(OH)_2} + \mathrm{H_2}$. (Mg reacts vigorously with **steam** to form MgO).
* **Hydroxides**: Solubility **increases** down the group.
    * $\mathrm{Mg(OH)_2}$: Insoluble (used in antacids).
    * $\mathrm{Ba(OH)_2}$: Soluble.
* **Sulfates**: Solubility **decreases** down the group.
    * $\mathrm{BaSO_4}$: Insoluble (used in 'Barium Meals' for gut imaging).

### 7.3 Testing for Sulfates
* Reagent: **Acidified Barium Chloride** ($\mathrm{BaCl_2}$ with $\mathrm{HCl}$).
* Positive Result: **White precipitate** of $\mathrm{BaSO_4}$.

## 8. ðŸ§ª Group 7 (The Halogens) (3.2.3)

### 8.1 Trends Down the Group
* **Boiling Point**: **Increases** (Larger molecules $\rightarrow$ stronger Van der Waals forces).
* **Electronegativity**: **Decreases** (Larger radius, more shielding).
* **Oxidising Ability**: **Decreases** (Harder to gain an electron).
    * $\mathrm{Cl_2}$ displaces $\mathrm{Br^-}$ and $\mathrm{I^-}$.
    * $\mathrm{Br_2}$ displaces $\mathrm{I^-}$.

### 8.2 Reducing Ability of Halide Ions
* **Trend**: Increases down the group ($\mathrm{I^-}$ is the strongest reducing agent).
* **Reaction with Conc. $\mathrm{H_2SO_4}$**:
    * **$\mathrm{F^-, Cl^-}$**: Acid-base only. Steamy fumes ($\mathrm{HCl/HF}$).
    * **$\mathrm{Br^-}$**: Acid-base + Redox. Fumes ($\mathrm{HBr}$) + Orange vapour ($\mathrm{Br_2}$) + $\mathrm{SO_2}$.
    * **$\mathrm{I^-}$**: Acid-base + Strong Redox. Fumes ($\mathrm{HI}$) + Purple vapour ($\mathrm{I_2}$) + Rotten egg smell ($\mathrm{H_2S}$).

### 8.3 Testing for Halide Ions
1.  Add **Dilute Nitric Acid** ($\mathrm{HNO_3}$) to remove Carbonates/Hydroxides.
2.  Add **Silver Nitrate** ($\mathrm{AgNO_3}$).
3.  **Observations**:
    * **$\mathrm{Cl^-}$**: White ppt (dissolves in dilute $\mathrm{NH_3}$).
    * **$\mathrm{Br^-}$**: Cream ppt (dissolves in conc $\mathrm{NH_3}$).
    * **$\mathrm{I^-}$**: Yellow ppt (insoluble in conc $\mathrm{NH_3}$).

### 8.4 Uses of Chlorine
* **Water Treatment**: $\mathrm{Cl_2 + H_2O \rightleftharpoons HCl + HClO}$. (HClO kills bacteria).
* **Bleach Formation**: $\mathrm{2NaOH + Cl_2 \rightarrow NaCl + NaClO + H_2O}$ (Cold, dilute alkali).
